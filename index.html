Samuel Cristobal

Software Engineer

In 1989, I got my first computer a Sinclair Spectrum ZX +2, a few years later an all mighty 386. I wrote my first programs in QBasic, and later learned C and Turbo Pascal.

In the 2000s, I studied a double degree in Mathematics and Engineering. Did my masters and worked in assistant positions in the algebra and logic departments across Europe.

During 2010, I was working as a R&D engineer, in between software and theory, I specialized in large scale graph dynamics optimization, resilience and discovery.

In 2019, I co-founded a B2B startup and joined as the CTO. The mission is to streamline air traffic management tools. I bootstrapped the initial product and build the engineering team.

I enjoy programming challenges, like Leet Code or Advent of Code. Also graphics and physics engines, algorithms and data structures, memory management, language and type theory and everything in between.

My favorite language is Rust and I am most exited about Web Assembly, WebGPU and Web Components.
